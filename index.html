Our Approach

Head, Heart & Hands

Holistic, nature-based education rooted in Waldorf traditions — nurturing every part of who a child is becoming.

thinkingHead

Imaginative academics that build curiosity, critical thinking, and a deep love of learning — at a pace that honours childhood.

feelingHeart

Strong relationships, emotional intelligence, music, story and reverence for the rhythms of the natural world.

doingHands

Real work — gardening, baking, woodwork, handcrafts — building competence, capability, and confidence in the body.

Our Story

A peaceful woodland classroom on an organic farm.

Cedar Grove is a small, not-for-profit school where the day moves with the weather, the season, and the child in front of us. Mornings might begin with a forest walk, an animal to feed, or bread to bake — and unfold into reading, painting, math and music.

"Where childhood grows naturally — and learning happens with the whole self."

We blend academics with creativity, movement, and a strong connection to nature, fostering curiosity, confidence, and a lifelong love of learning.

A Day Here

Days that breathe with rhythm.

Each day moves between focused academic work and time outdoors — between stillness and movement, doing and resting. Children know what to expect, and within that rhythm, they're free to wonder.

8:30 — Arrival

Morning circle & verse

Greeting the day together with song, movement and a moment of quiet.

9:00 — Main Lesson

Deep academic work

A two-hour block on one subject — math, language, history or science — taught through story and image.

11:00 — Outdoors

Forest, farm & play

Free play, animal care, gardening, and exploration of the woods around the school.

12:30 — Lunch

A shared, unhurried meal

Often featuring food the children helped prepare or harvest themselves.

1:30 — Afternoon

Arts, crafts & practical work

Painting, handwork, baking, music, French — the work of the hands and the heart.

3:15 — Closing

A grateful goodbye

A quiet ending and a verse to carry home.

Land Acknowledgment

We learn on borrowed land.

Cedar Grove School recognizes that we live, learn, and gather on the traditional territory of the Anishnaabeg, Wendat, and Haudenosaunee peoples, and near the Kanien’keha:ka (Mohawk) community of Tyendinaga. We honour their deep, enduring relationship with this land and express our gratitude for their ongoing care and stewardship.

We are committed to fostering respectful partnerships and to protecting the land and water for generations to come.

— with gratitude
