The Team

The teachers and staff who care for the children, the land, and the daily life of Cedar Grove.

M

Marissa Norton

School Administrator

Marissa Norton is an Ontario Certified Teacher and experienced educational leader with more than twenty years of experience supporting students, families, and educators across a wide range of learning environments. She brings a deep commitment to whole-child education, relationship-based leadership, and fostering school communities where every child feels seen, valued, and empowered.

She holds a Bachelor of Education from Queen’s University and an Honours Bachelor of Arts in Kinesiology and Health Science from York University. Her background spans elementary and secondary education, adult education, educational advocacy, academic consulting, and school administration. Before joining Cedar Grove School as School Administrator, Marissa served for nine years as Principal of a private therapeutic school, where she led a collaborative team dedicated to supporting students with diverse learning needs.

Marissa’s professional training includes Therapeutic Crisis Intervention, trauma-informed practice, and special education. Her educational philosophy is rooted in recognizing the unique strengths of every child and creating environments where curiosity, confidence, and a love of learning can flourish.

Originally from Toronto and raised in Prince Edward County, Marissa is grateful to be serving the community she calls home. Outside of school, she enjoys spending time in nature with her two teenage children and is continually inspired by creativity, lifelong learning, and the individuality that emerges when children are given the freedom to explore the world with imagination.

Brittany Shannon

Lead Grade School Teacher & Pedagogical Director

Brittany Shannon is a Waldorf-trained educator, artist, and educational leader devoted to whole-child development and nature-based learning. She has been actively involved in developing a Waldorf initiative in Prince Edward County since 2011.

She holds a Waldorf Teacher Certification and Foundation Studies in Anthroposophy from Rudolf Steiner College Canada, along with a Bachelor of Education from Queen’s University and a Bachelor of Arts (Honours) in Visual Arts from the University of Ottawa. Her background also includes studies in biodynamic agriculture, storytelling, and experiential education.

Brittany previously served as a Class Teacher at Mulberry Waldorf School in Kingston, guiding students through multiple grades as a Main Lesson teacher. She later founded and operated a Waldorf-inspired daycare and developed eco-arts programming in Prince Edward County, integrating handwork, storytelling, seasonal festivals, and outdoor exploration into children’s daily lives.

As Lead Grade School Teacher and Pedagogical Director of Cedar Grove School, Brittany supports curriculum development, mentors colleagues, and helps steward the school’s educational vision. Her teaching is rooted in artistic practice, meaningful work, and a deep respect for childhood.

Brittany is also a wife and mother of two sons, and she brings her lived experience of family life into her work with warmth, steadiness, and deep care for the children and families she serves.

Kerri (Kaya’tahente) Smart

Lead Teacher, Kindergarten & Grade 1

Kerri Smart is a Bear Clan woman from Tyendinaga Mohawk Territory and a member of the Ontario College of Teachers. She is also known by her Indigenous name, Kaya’tahente (pronounced guy-yut-dah-hon-day), which translates to “leader” in the Mohawk language. She brings over fifteen years of experience in early childhood education, along with a background in educational administration.

Kerri holds a Mohawk Language Diploma and a Bachelor of Arts in Indigenous Studies from Trent University, as well as a Bachelor of Education and a professional Master of Education from Queen’s University. In 2025, she became a certified Forest and Nature School practitioner, deepening her commitment to land-based and nature-connected learning.

Rooted in Waldorf-inspired pedagogy, Kerri weaves storytelling, rhythm, nature, culture and handwork into her teaching practice, creating warm and nurturing spaces where children can grow with wonder and confidence. As a mother to a young son and a wife of eighteen years, she brings the heart of family life into her work with children.

She is passionate about lifelong learning, meaningful teaching, and spending time outdoors, and she delights in creating cozy, peaceful environments where children feel safe, seen, and inspired.

Jasmin Wakeling

Assistant Teacher

Jaz is thrilled to be joining Cedar Grove as an Assistant Teacher. She brings a positive attitude, a passion for all things nature, and experience working with children in both outdoor and educational settings. Jaz is excited to share her knowledge of the natural world, helping children discover the incredible diversity of species, the interconnectedness of ecosystems, and the importance of caring for our planet, each other, and ourselves.

Jaz graduated with Honours from Trent University with a degree in Environmental Resource Science. Through her own experiences with experiential learning, exploration, and taking thoughtful risks, she developed a deep appreciation for Waldorf-inspired education and its emphasis on curiosity, creativity, and connection with nature.

In her personal life, Jaz spends as much time outdoors as possible, often exploring new trails with her dog or listening for unfamiliar bird calls. She feels truly grateful to be a part of the Cedar Grove community and is excited to support each child’s growth, confidence, and love of learning.

Kelly Purves

Teaching Assistant & Administrative Assistant

Kelly is a lifelong learner and dedicated environmental steward with a history rooted in the natural world. She supports Cedar Grove School through both administration and student learning, bringing a grounded presence to the school’s daily operations and living classroom.

Kelly’s expertise is built on three decades of work in conservation and sustainable farming. She studied Environmental Technology at Algonquin College and Food Security at TMU, and has a passion for botany. Her practice is enriched by her background facilitating outdoor experiences for families and youth, as well as her training as a yoga practitioner for both children and adults.

Kelly’s two nature-raised boys have been her greatest teachers, inspiring her to live more intuitively. On this path she has learned to advocate for fueling the body with whole foods, cultivating inner awareness, and weaving movement into everyday life.

To Kelly, being in the presence of children is sweet magic. She believes in creating spaces where wonder is the curriculum and children can develop a lasting connection with the wild world.

Claire Telford

Co-Founder

Claire is a Co-Founder of Cedar Grove School, bringing a deep commitment to community and to the vision that shaped the school from its earliest days.

She holds an Honours Bachelor of Science from the University of Toronto and spent five years studying and practicing homeopathic medicine, deepening her understanding of whole-person wellness and the interconnectedness of body, mind, and environment — values that resonate strongly with Waldorf education.

Earlier in her career, she lived and worked in Japan as an English teacher, where she also studied Japanese language and culture, broadening her perspective on education as a bridge between people and communities.

Claire is the founder of Telford, a local personal care line rooted in simplicity and care. Her entrepreneurial spirit brings creativity, initiative, and thoughtful stewardship to her leadership at Cedar Grove. A mother of three and partner to Nick Sorbara, whose family stewards the land Cedar Grove calls home, Claire is deeply connected to the school’s place and purpose and is devoted to nurturing a grounded, thriving community for children and families alike.
